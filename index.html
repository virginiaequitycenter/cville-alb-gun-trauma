Charlottesville & Albemarle Gun Violence

The information below aims to describe the nature, scope, and dimensions of the multifaceted problem of gun violence locally. Part of the Gun Violence Solutions Project, this research emphasizes community-driven partnerships to develop a shared understanding and greater clarity about the local problem of gun violence, identify knowledge and data gaps, and facilitate wider community engagement in conversations about solutions.

Policy Brief

The Problem of Community Gun Violence

Healthy communities thrive when all members experience physical and psychological safety. Gun violence increasingly threatens the health of our communities — particularly for youth and young adults.

The Problem of Unintentional Shootings

Unintentional shootings, sometimes called accidental shootings,4 occur when someone is struck by a bullet that wasn’t fired at them on purpose.
